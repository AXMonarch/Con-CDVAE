THE PROBLEM: Lack of Interpretability in Black Box Models

Crystal generation models operate as black boxes with limited mechanistic understanding. Existing VAE architectures fail to provide clear causal pathways between input conditions and generated outputs, making it impossible to understand why specific crystal structures are generated or how formation energy influences the generation process.

OUR SOLUTION: Mechanistically Interpretable Conditional VAE

Con-CDVAE achieves mechanistic interpretability through a transparent causal bottleneck (z_cond). We enable precise understanding by: (1) Neural Activation Tracing: Every neuron's contribution is trackable from input formation energy through the conditioning pathway to final predictions. (2) Causal Intervention: By patching z_cond vectors between crystals, we demonstrated that this 256-dimensional bottleneck causally controls ALL outputs (lattice, composition, atom types). (3) Transparent Information Flow: The fusion of structural encoding (z_mu) with formation energy conditioning creates an interpretable pathway where we can identify exactly which neurons encode thermodynamic vs. structural information.
